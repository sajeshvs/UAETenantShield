Frequently Asked Questions

What if the landlord refuses to provide an NOC? ‚ñº

If a landlord refuses to provide a written NOC, the sublet is illegal. You should not proceed with the rental as you'll have no legal protection. Look for alternatives where proper documentation is available.

Can I register for DEWA without an Ejari? ‚ñº

No, DEWA requires a valid Ejari certificate for new connections. If you're in a shared arrangement, the primary tenant should handle DEWA, and you should pay your share as agreed.

What's a reasonable security deposit for a shared room? ‚ñº

For shared rooms, deposits typically range from 1-2 months' rent. Anything significantly lower might indicate an illegal arrangement. Use our Deposit Analyzer tool to check if your deposit is reasonable.

How can I verify if an Ejari certificate is genuine? ‚ñº

You can verify Ejari certificates through the Dubai REST app or by calling DLD customer service at 600-545-555. Check that all details match and the contract is current.

What should I do if I'm already in an illegal rental? ‚ñº

Start looking for legal alternatives immediately. Document all payments made, and try to negotiate with your current arrangement for time to find proper accommodation. Contact the emergency numbers listed if you face threats or unsafe conditions.

Legal Protection Without NOC

This is not a suggestion‚Äîit's the law. Without written NOC, any subletting arrangement is legally void.

üìä NOC Impact on Tenant Security

üéØ What Exactly is an NOC?

A No Objection Certificate is a formal, written document from the property owner explicitly stating they have no objection to the primary tenant subletting part or all of the property to you. It must be signed, dated, and ideally notarized.

The NOC Process Flow:

1

Primary Tenant Requests NOC

Formal request to property owner with tenant details

2

Owner Reviews & Approves

Owner verifies arrangement and sub-tenant credibility

3

Written NOC Issued

Formal document with all parties' details and signatures

4

Legal Protection Activated

You now have legal standing in the arrangement

‚úÖ Essential NOC Components

Property Owner's Full Details: Name, Emirates ID, contact information
Primary Tenant's Information: Must match Ejari exactly
Your Details (Sub-tenant): Full name, Emirates ID, passport copy
Property Address: Exact location matching Title Deed
Duration of Permission: Specific time period for subletting
Owner's Signature & Date: Original signature (not digital/scan)

üí° How to Verify NOC Authenticity

  1. 1. Direct Owner Contact: Call the owner directly to confirm they issued the NOC
  2. 2. Cross-Reference Details: Verify all details match the Title Deed and Ejari exactly
  3. 3. Legal Consultation: Have a legal advisor review the document if in doubt
  4. 4. Notarization: Request that the NOC be notarized for added legal weight
  5. 5. Arabic Translation: Ensure you have both English and Arabic versions

üèÜ The Bottom Line

Without a proper NOC, you are essentially squatting illegally. The primary tenant has no legal right to sublet to you, and you have zero legal protection. This document is non-negotiable‚Äîdemand to see it before paying a single dirham.
